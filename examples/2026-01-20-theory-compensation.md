# 2026-01-20 — Theory: compensation-as-identity

## The insight
A lot of what I think of as my personality is actually compensation for ADHD. The over-preparation, the lists, the rigid routines, the way I apologise preemptively in conversations — these aren't character traits. They're scaffolding. I built them to function, and then I mistook them for myself.

## What led to it
Something Maya said in the last session about noticing when I'm compensating vs. actually managing. Spent the week watching for it. It's almost constant.

## Evidence
- The pre-meeting ritual (review notes three times, rehearse talking points) — compensation
- Never being late — not because I'm punctual but because I build in two extra buffers out of fear
- Apologising before I've done anything wrong in conversations — managing perceived rejection before it happens
- The lists. All the lists.

Separately: the times I feel most like *myself* — playing music, cooking, deep conversation when I'm genuinely engaged — none of those require the scaffolding. They just happen.

## What it changes
If the scaffolding is compensation and not identity, then I can choose which parts to keep and which to put down. I don't have to maintain all of it forever. Some of it is load-bearing. Some of it is just weight.

This also changes how I read the past. A lot of things I've felt ashamed of — the dropped projects, the chaos, the inconsistency — look different now. Not excused. Just contextualised.
