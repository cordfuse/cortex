# 2026-01-08 — Diagnosis

## What happened
Got the results from the assessment today. ADHD — combined type. The psychiatrist walked me through the report for about 45 minutes.

I expected to feel relieved. I do, a little. But there's also something harder to name. Like grief, almost. For all the years of not knowing.

## How it felt
The moment she said it I just thought: *that's why*. That's why the job in 2019 fell apart. That's why I can't finish things. That's why the relationships. It all made sense at once and that felt huge — and then two minutes later I was sitting in the car not knowing what to do with it.

Cried a bit on the drive home. Not sad exactly. Just a release of something that had been held a long time.

## What led to it
Referred by my GP after I brought it up in October. Assessment took three months. Two sessions plus a written questionnaire and a report from someone who knew me as a kid.

## What came after
Told Sam when I got home. Sat with it together for a while. That helped. Sam didn't try to fix anything — just listened.

## What it means
I don't know yet. The psychiatrist mentioned medication as an option, not a requirement. We're meeting again in three weeks to talk about next steps.

What I keep coming back to: this doesn't change anything that happened. But it changes how I understand it.
