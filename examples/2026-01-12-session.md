# 2026-01-12 — Session: Maya (therapist)

## Provider
Maya — therapist, fortnightly. Been seeing her about eight months.

## What I brought in
The diagnosis from last week. Wanted to process it with someone outside my immediate circle.

## What we covered
Spent most of the session on the grief angle. Maya asked me to describe what I was grieving specifically. I struggled — it's not a person or an event. She suggested it might be the version of myself that would have existed with the right support earlier. That landed hard.

We also talked about the pattern of finishing things. She connected it to the avoidance work we did in September — suggested the ADHD might be upstream of a lot of what we've been treating as anxiety.

## What she said
"The diagnosis doesn't rewrite who you are. It adds a chapter of context to a story that was already yours."

Held onto that.

## What I realised
I've been treating the ADHD as a new problem to solve. Maya reframed it as context, not a task list. That was useful.

Also: I've been minimising how much energy I spend compensating. She pointed out I've built a lot of scaffolding over the years. That scaffolding is real skill — it's not fake. The diagnosis doesn't take it away.

## What to carry forward
- Sit with the grief without trying to resolve it immediately
- Start noticing when I'm compensating vs. actually managing
- Bring the work/disclosure question to next session
